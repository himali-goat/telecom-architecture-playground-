1. Strategy, Infrastructure & Product (SIP)
Build the business: strategy, products, services, resources, and supply chain direction.
L1

Strategy & Commit

Enterprise direction, market positioning, planning priorities, and major business commitments.

Infrastructure Lifecycle Management

Plan, build, and evolve infrastructure capabilities needed for service delivery.

Product Lifecycle Management

Define, design, launch, enhance, and retire product offers.

Service Lifecycle Management

Design and maintain customer-facing and resource-facing services.

Resource Lifecycle Management

Manage lifecycle of technical resources, platforms, and enabling assets over time.

Supply Chain Development & Management

Manage vendors, sourcing strategy, procurement relationships, and supply readiness.

2.2 Assurance
Monitor, detect, analyze, resolve, and improve issues affecting customers and services.
L2

Customer Assurance

Customer care, complaint handling, trouble intake, communication, and resolution updates (TMF: Customer Problem Handling).

Service Assurance

Service quality management, service problem handling, SLA management, and performance control. (TMF: Service Quality + Service Problem Management)

Resource Assurance

Fault management, technical diagnostics, resource performance, and restoration activities. (TMF: Resource Trouble + Performance Management)

Partner / Supplier Assurance

Issue coordination and service quality management with external providers and partners. (TMF: Partner / Supplier Problem Management)

›
2.3 Billing
Charge, invoice, collect, settle, and manage financial interactions tied to telecom services.
L2

Customer Billing

Invoice generation, payment handling, balance management, credits, disputes, and collections. (TMF: Billing and Collections)

Service Charging and Rating

Usage measurement, rating inputs, recurring service charge management, and charge integrity. (TMF: Rating and Charging)

Resource Usage and Mediation

Usage capture, mediation, and billable event generation. (TMF: Usage Capture / Mediation / Billable Event Generation)

Partner / Supplier Settlement

Intercompany charging, settlement, partner invoicing, and supplier financial reconciliation. (TMF: Partner Settlement & Interconnect Billing)
